# Commercial Licensing

wInstaller is free to use, share, modify, and fork for **any noncommercial
purpose** under the [wInstaller Noncommercial License](LICENSE.md) (PolyForm
Noncommercial 1.0.0 plus a noncommercial-copyleft addendum).

If you want to use wInstaller — or a modified/derivative version of it — for a
**commercial purpose**, you need a separate, paid, negotiated license from the
developer. This document explains what that means and how to ask for one.

## What counts as commercial use

In plain language, commercial use is anything primarily intended for, or
directed toward, commercial advantage or monetary compensation. Some examples
(non-exhaustive):

- Bundling wInstaller (or a fork of it) into a paid product, a managed
  service, or an IT support offering you sell or charge for.
- Using wInstaller internally at a for-profit company as part of a paid
  service you deliver to customers.
- Distributing a modified version of wInstaller under a different name as
  part of a commercial offering.

Personal use, hobby use, use by charitable/educational/public-research/public-
safety/government organizations, and use for public-interest research or
study are **not** commercial use under this project's license — see
[LICENSE.md](LICENSE.md) for the exact (legally controlling) definitions.

If you're not sure whether your use case counts, ask — see below.

## How to request a commercial license

Email **pedrohbrito@me.com** with:

1. A short description of how you (or your company) want to use wInstaller.
2. Whether you plan to modify or redistribute it as part of your product.
3. Roughly how many users/seats/installs this would cover.

You'll get a response with either a straightforward answer (many uses turn out
not to require a commercial license at all) or a proposed license and terms.
There's no fixed price list — commercial terms are negotiated case-by-case
based on scope of use.

## Why this model

wInstaller is built to be genuinely useful and freely available to anyone —
individuals, students, IT professionals, nonprofits, schools — for free,
forever, including the right to fork and modify it (as long as forks stay
under the same noncommercial terms, so the guarantee can't be stripped out
downstream). Commercial use funds continued development directly instead of
through telemetry, ads, or a freemium paywall inside the app itself — both of
which conflict with this project's local-only, no-telemetry trust model (see
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) and [VISION.md](docs/specs/VISION.md)).
